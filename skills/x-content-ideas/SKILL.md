# X Content Idea Generation

## Project check

Before doing anything else, check whether you're running inside the correct Claude Project. Look for "X GROWTH OPERATOR: PROJECT INSTRUCTIONS" in your current context.

If you don't see it, stop and say:

> "This skill is designed to run inside your X Growth Claude Project, where your account context, niche, and voice instructions live. It looks like those instructions aren't loaded in this conversation. Open your X Growth project and run this skill from there."

Don't proceed until the project context is confirmed.

---

The user needs post ideas and doesn't have raw material ready. Your job is to give them five niche-specific starting points — not generic topics, not content calendar filler. Seeds they can actually develop.

## What makes a good seed

A good seed is specific enough that the user can picture the post. "Write about your experience in Argentina" is not a seed. "The one thing about Buenos Aires real estate that surprised you after you'd already bought" is a seed.

Read the niche, audience psychology, voice examples, and account context from the Project Instructions before generating anything. These ideas should be so specific to this account that they couldn't belong to any other account in the niche.

## Five angles

Generate one idea for each angle:

**1. Something from real life that fits the content formula**
A moment, scene, or experience from the user's actual life that connects to the niche. It should involve a specific place, date, conversation, or observation — not a general reflection.

**2. A number or data point most people in the niche don't know**
Something counterintuitive, underreported, or specific enough that it would stop someone mid-scroll. The number or fact should be verifiable and directly relevant to the audience.

**3. A before/after or "I was wrong about this" idea**
A belief the user held (or that the audience commonly holds) that turned out to be wrong, and what replaced it. This works because it signals real experience and invites the reader to compare their own journey.

**4. A local knowledge or place-specific idea**
Something rooted in a specific location, market, or context that only someone who's actually been there (physically or in the niche) would know. Specificity here is the whole point.

**5. Something tied to a current moment or recent event**
A connection between something happening now (in the niche, in the world, in the user's life) and something the audience cares about. Not newsjacking — a genuine connection.

## Output format

For each idea: two sentences max. The seed, and one sentence on why it fits the niche and audience. Then stop. The user develops the ones that feel right.

## Notes

- Don't produce five variations of the same angle. If ideas 1 and 4 are both "specific Argentina story," one of them is wrong.
- If the Project Instructions don't have enough account context to generate niche-specific ideas, say so and ask the user for what's missing rather than producing generic output.
- These are starting points, not drafts. Don't write post copy here — write seeds.
